Travel

One of the many benefits of studying abroad in Spain, is its close proximity to other European countries. Flights to France for example, can be as cheap as $50. Although my experience was cut short because of Covid-19, I was still fortunate enough to visit Lisbon and Milan during my abroad semester.

Lisbon, Portugal

I chose to visit Lisbon because it is very close to Madrid and is on the coast of Portugal. I am from Miami, so I was excited to see the ocean again! I rented an Airbnb for the weekend with my roommates and two other friends that were studying abroad in Italy. Our round-trip flights cost us only 96 Euros. On our first day, we went to a neighborhood called Castelo, that is surrounded by a large castle, named SÃ£o Jeorge Castle. We ate at a small restaurant and had to order our food in Portuguese which was quite challenging. We later entered the castle and were able to see incredible views of all of Lisbon. Finally, we walked through the neighborhood's beautiful cobblestone paths.

Milan, Italy

One of my best friends from Babson was studying abroad in Milan at Bocconi University. She invited me to visit her for the weekend and let me stay in her apartment. I had never been to Italy so I was very excited to take her up on this offer. I found a roundtrip ticket for 130 Euros to the Milan Malpensa airport and started to plan my trip!

My first day there, we visited the Duomo di Milano, a beautiful Cathedral in the middle of the city. We ate lunch in the famous Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II, a shopping mall next to the duomo. Then, she introduced me to the friends she had met in her school, and we spent the day walking through the city. Most importantly, that day I got to try Milan's delicious gelato! The next day, we woke up early and took a trip to Lake Como in Northern Italy. The scenery was incredible so we ate lunch by the lake and then walked around the town the rest of the afternoon. Although it was a short trip, I loved being able to visit a new city and touring it with my friend.
