My Experience abroad

The Application Process

As a first-semester junior in college, I was still unsure of whether I wanted to go abroad or not. Growing up, I never went to sleep away camps or had any experience with leaving home for such a long period of time, other than college. Everyone around me was telling me that I could not miss this opportunity to study abroad but I was unsure if I could last an entire semester in a different country. I spoke with the study abroad counselors and they recommended that I begin to research different countries, schools, and programs in order to familiarize myself with the options I had.

I had never traveled to Europe, so I knew that this would be a great opportunity to do so. However, there were so many options to choose from that I was overwhelmed pretty quickly. The first country I was interested in was Italy because I had heard of how beautiful Florence was that it was a small enough city where I would feel comfortable within my first few weeks. However, as I learned more about the educational programs, they did not really match the classes that I wanted to take abroad. While they offered many liberal arts classes, I was looking to take business courses.

Then, I began to look into the differences between doing an exchange program through another University, and going with an American program with many students from different schools. The study abroad counselor mentioned to me that the rigor of an exchange program was much higher, and that I would get to meet people from the city I was living in, instead of other Americans. I came to the conclusion that this was the best option for me, which really got me excited to choose a new city and school.

I then looked into Spain, because my family is originally from Colombia so I speak fluent Spanish. I thought this would be a great country to go to because I might find comfort knowing that I will be able to communicate easily even if I am in a country I have never been before. I narrowed it down to Madrid or Barcelona, but ultimately decided on Madrid because of the IE University's prestigious academics, and its close proximity to the city.

How I found housing

I was lucky enough to get accepted into IE University with one of my best friends from Babson. We decided we would try to find an apartment together with one of her friends from her home town, who would also be studying abroad in Madrid. We used many companies to find long-term rentals, such as Idealista and Homeclub. After a couple of weeks, we found the perfect 3-bedroom apartment within our budget. It was 7 minute bus ride to IE University, and a 10 minute drive to the city. Living in a centralized area really made our experience incredible because we had access to all different types of transportation, such as the metro and bus, but could also walk to most places. We discovered many nice restaurants, parks and bars within walking distance to our apartment which made a new city very "homey". All that was left to do was choose my classes!
